Risotto El Fungi

"A Fungalicious Risotto Recipe"

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth, kept warm
  • 1 cup finely chopped onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 oz (about 225g) mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, and oyster), sliced
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Mushrooms: In a large skillet or pan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and sauté until they are golden brown and any liquid released has evaporated. Set aside.
  2. Sauté Onions and Garlic: In the same pan, add another tablespoon of olive oil. Add the chopped onions and garlic, sauté until they are soft and translucent.
  3. Toast Rice: Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes until the rice is lightly toasted.
  4. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the dry white wine, stirring constantly until the wine is mostly absorbed.
  5. Add Broth: Begin adding the warm broth, one ladle at a time. Allow the liquid to be absorbed before adding the next ladle. Stir frequently.
  6. Continue Cooking: Continue adding broth and stirring until the rice is creamy and cooked to al dente, which usually takes about 18-20 minutes.
  7. Incorporate Mushrooms: Stir in the sautéed mushrooms during the last 5 minutes of cooking.
  8. Finish with Butter and Cheese: Once the rice is cooked, remove the pan from heat. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Garnish and Serve: Garnish the risotto with chopped fresh parsley. Serve the mushroom risotto immediately, drizzling with a little extra olive oil if desired.
